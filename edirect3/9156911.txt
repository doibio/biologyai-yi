Imidazoline-preferring receptors are important in the pathophysiology of 
hypertension. The selective l1-receptor agonists are moxonidine, cimetidine and 
rilmenidine. Some clinical studies indicate the usefulness of moxonidine therapy 
in hypertension, arrhythmias and acute myocardial infarction.
