1. Everolimus.

Drugs and Lactation Database (LactMed®) [Internet]. Bethesda (MD): National 
Institute of Child Health and Human Development; 2006–.
2023 Oct 15.

In two women, everolimus was either undetectable or detectable in very small 
amounts in the colostrum. However, no information is available on the use of 
everolimus during breastfeeding. An alternate drug may be preferred, especially 
while nursing a newborn or preterm infant.
